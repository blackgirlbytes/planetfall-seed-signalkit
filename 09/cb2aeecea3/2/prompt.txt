Yeah, I think you found the actual issue: **the cars are already on their tracks**, so the player is mostly just starting a machine.

Dragging could fix that, but only if dragging means **choosing**, not just moving pieces around.

I’d try this:

**Level 2 = Dispatch Grid**
Cars start in a bay at the bottom. Tracks are empty. Each track leads to a broken system.

Player loop:

1. **Drag a subagent car onto a track**
2. **Press Space to launch assigned cars**
3. **Use one boost/problem moment while they run**
4. **Review checkpoint, add to ship**

The key is: tracks should have problems.

Example track problems:

- **Static Gate**: opens/closes, launch timing matters.
- **Corruption Patch**: slows the car unless boosted through it.
- **Locked Track**: needs the Decoder car.
- **Broken Track**: needs the Repair car.
- **Signal Fog**: needs the Scout/Research car.
- **Firewall Track**: needs the Security car.

So dragging becomes meaningful:

> “This track is locked. I should drag Decoder here.”

My favorite simple version:

**Five cars. Five tracks. One visible problem per track.**
Each car has a specialty icon. Each track has a matching problem icon. You drag cars into the lanes, then launch them.

During the run, each lane has **one boost pad**. When the car reaches it, click the lane to fire nitro. That gives you something to do after launch without making it chaotic.

So the game has three satisfying verbs:

- **Drag** to assign
- **Launch** to start
- **Boost** to help

That feels way better than only “click all cars and wait.” are we able to do parallel or sequential..like i can put as many cars on the track as i want??  Yes, exactly. That should be the choice.

I’d make it:

**Cars start in the bay. Tracks are empty.**  
You drag as many cars onto tracks as you want, then launch.

So the player can choose:

**Sequential**
Drag 1 car to 1 track → launch → manage boost/problem → review.  
Safer, easier, but slow.

**Parallel**
Drag 3-5 cars onto tracks → launch them all together.  
Faster, but now you’re juggling boost moments / track problems at the same time.

Best rule: **one car per track**, but you can fill any number of tracks before launching.

That makes the decision interesting:

> “Do I send one car and play it safe, or send all five and try to manage the chaos?”

The current Level 2 already kind of has this idea because you can arm one car or arm all five before pressing Space. But dragging would make that choice feel way more physical and intentional.

The button/prompt could say:

- `1 CAR STAGED`
- `3 CARS STAGED`
- `SPACE: LAUNCH 3`
- `DRAG CARS TO TRACKS`

That’s probably the missing fun: not just watching cars go, but choosing how much parallel work you dare to run at once.
